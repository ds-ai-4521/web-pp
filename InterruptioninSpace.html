Interruption in Space

1977.

"Interruption in Space" refers to a performance art piece by Marina Abramović and Ulay (Uwe Laysiepen). The work was part of their collaborative performances during the 1970s, a period when they were romantically involved and created a series of innovative and boundary-pushing works exploring the dynamics of relationships. It serves as a sequel to her 1976 piece, "Relation in Space"



In the aformentioned art piece, which took place in 1977, Abramović and Ulay explored the theme of interruption and disruption within the context of their shared artistic and personal relationship. The performance involved the artists standing in a narrow passageway, their bodies pressed against the walls on either side, creating a physical barrier. The audience was invited to pass through this confined space, forcing them to physically interrupt the intimate connection between Abramović and Ulay.



As people moved through the passage, they became active participants in the performance, disrupting the personal space of the artists and confronting the tension between the public and private aspects of their relationship. The work addressed the themes of boundaries, intrusion, and the impact of external forces on personal connections.




"Interruption in Space" is an example of Abramović and Ulay's exploration of the relationship between performers and audience, as well as their interest in using their own bodies to convey complex emotional and conceptual ideas. The piece challenges the traditional passive role of the spectator by directly involving the audience in the creation and experience of the artwork.
