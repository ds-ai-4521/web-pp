Balkan Baroque

1997.

"Balkan Baroque" by Marina Abramović is a deeply introspective and emotionally charged performance that delves into the artist's personal history and the complex socio-political landscape of the Balkans.

The use of cow bones in it serves as a powerful symbol. Abramović chose the bones to represent both the agricultural roots of the Balkans and the aftermath of the violent conflicts that plagued the region, particularly during the breakup of Yugoslavia in the 1990s. The repetitive act of cleaning the bones becomes a ritualistic and meditative process, reflecting the need for cleansing and reconciliation in the face of historical trauma.



As Abramović meticulously cleaned each bone, she recited stories and accounts from her own life, connecting personal experiences with the broader cultural and political context. The inclusion of her autobiography adds a layer of intimacy to the performance, inviting the audience to witness the artist's vulnerability and engage with the complexities of identity, memory, and resilience.




The piece was performed daily for a total of four days, each session lasting several hours. Abramović's endurance and commitment to the physically demanding task of cleaning each bone contributed to the visceral impact of the performance. The extended duration allowed for a profound immersion into the work, amplifying the emotional and psychological resonance for both the artist and the audience.

Audience members were an integral part of the performance. They were invited to witness Abramović's actions up close, creating an intimate and participatory experience. The emotional weight of the stories shared, combined with the physicality of the bone-cleaning process, had a profound impact on the viewers, eliciting a range of emotions from empathy to discomfort.


It is considered one of Marina Abramović's seminal works, showcasing her ability to blend personal narrative with broader social and political themes. The piece has left a lasting impact on the contemporary art scene, prompting discussions about the role of performance art in addressing historical traumas and the ways in which artists can navigate their own cultural and political identities.



While the live performances of "Balkan Baroque" were ephemeral, the piece has been documented through photographs and video recordings. These archival materials contribute to the ongoing legacy of the work, allowing audiences to engage with its themes and Abramović's powerful presence even after the original performances have concluded.


"Balkan Baroque" stands as a testament to Marina Abramović's ability to use her body and personal experiences as a canvas for exploring broader societal issues, leaving a lasting imprint on the history of performance art.


The piece also shares a name with the 1999 documentary, which serves as a biographical look into Abramović's life. The film composes the life aesthetic of a woman in her era, with a personal history strongly marked by the Yugoslavia of Tito, everyday violence, the experience of physical and psychic limits.
