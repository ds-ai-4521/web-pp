Shoes for Departure

1991.

"Shoes for Departure" is a work by Marina Abramović that was created in 1991. In this performance piece, Abramović explores themes of identity, separation, and departure in the context of her personal and cultural history.



In the piece Abramović invited the audience to participate by bringing a pair of shoes, placing them on a small platform, and then walking away. The act of leaving one's shoes behind symbolized a departure, suggesting the idea of migration, leaving a part of oneself behind, and the emotional weight associated with separation and movement.


The members of the audience actively contribute to the artwork by bringing a pair of shoes and placing them on a designated platform. The act of leaving one's shoes behind serves as a symbolic gesture.



Shoes, as personal items that come into direct contact with the body and the ground, carry a strong symbolic weight. They can represent aspects of identity, journeys, and the human experience. The collective presence of shoes in the performance creates a shared narrative.

The performance creates a shared ritualistic experience. Each pair of shoes left behind becomes a tangible representation of an individual's presence and departure, contributing to a collective narrative that transcends the specific stories of participants.


Like many of Abramović's works, "Shoes for Departure" likely has a temporal aspect where the performance unfolds over a specific period. The evolving arrangement of shoes and the changing landscape of the platform contribute to the overall experience.
