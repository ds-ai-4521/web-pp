The Artist is Present

2010.

"The Artist is Present" is a notable performance art piece by Marina Abramović, and it was also the title of a documentary that captures the artist's retrospective at the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA) in New York in 2010.



In aformentioned art piece, Marina Abramović sat in the museum's atrium for a total of 736 hours over the course of three months. Visitors to the museum were invited to sit across from her at a table and share a silent gaze. The performance was a powerful exploration of the connection between the artist and the audience, as well as an endurance test for Abramović herself.


The focal point of the performance was a simple wooden table and two chairs placed in the museum's atrium. Marina Abramović sat at the table, and visitors were invited to take turns sitting across from her.



The essence of "The Artist is Present" was the silent exchange that occurred between Abramović and each participant. The artist and the participant would share eye contact and sit in silence for an undetermined amount of time.



The performance had a significant impact on Marina Abramović as well. It required immense concentration and endurance, and the artist described the experience as both physically and emotionally demanding.


The documentary was directed by Matthew Akers and Jeff Dupre. It provides an in-depth look at Abramović's life and career leading up to and during her retrospective at MoMA. The film covers various aspects of her work, including her earlier performances, and explores the impact of "The Artist is Present" on both Abramović and the public.



The film delves into Abramović's personal history, her early life in Yugoslavia, and her evolution as a groundbreaking performance artist. It explores the motivations behind her work, her influences, and the challenges she faced as a pioneering figure in the art world.

"The Artist Is Present" also provides a glimpse into Marina Abramović's collaborations with fellow artists, including her former partner Ulay (Uwe Laysiepen). The film explores the impact of their artistic and personal relationship on Abramović's work and her approach to performance art.



One of the documentary's strengths is its ability to capture the emotional experiences of the participants who sat across from Abramović during the "The Artist is Present" performance. Viewers get to witness the profound and sometimes tearful interactions between Abramović and the individuals who took part in the silent exchange.



The film sheds light on Marina Abramović's artistic process, detailing the preparation and execution of her performances. It explores the physical and emotional demands placed on the artist as she engaged in lengthy and challenging performances throughout her career.


It ended up receiving widespread acclaim, not only for its portrayal of Abramović's work but also for its ability to communicate the transformative power of art. The documentary captures the attention the exhibition received from both the art community and the general public.


The documentary contributes to Marina Abramović's legacy by documenting a pivotal moment in her career and providing a comprehensive overview of her impact on the art world. It has become an important resource for those interested in contemporary performance art.
